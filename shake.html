Shake It Out

Shake It Out is a web app which utilises machine learning to encourage a burst of movement in 30 seconds.

Do you sit for hours, hunched over your screen? Are you struggling to focus? Do you need a quick rush of endorphins but can’t go outside? Shake It Out offers a playful and accessible solution to alleviate pain points associated with working from home.

23 March 2020, marks the beginning of the first lockdown in England. This personal project is a response to my growing frustration at being stuck inside my flat.

Process

I always refer to Henrik Kniberg’s metaphorical diagram when faced with a more complex task. The framework allows me to tackle broad problems in a rational and cohesive way.

My first step was to build a prototype with the mouse.

User Testing

Conducted remote & unmoderated user testing with the aim to find out if users prefer interacing with a timer or an increasing score count. A comparative approach informed me that users feel more motivated to wipe away screen bubbles with only the timer ticking. “I’m excited to see my score after competing against the clock”




Reflection and Next Steps

On building a chrome extension
Currently conducting research into designing interruption as a method to encourage exercise. A browser extension will allow users to integrate the app into their schedule more seamlessly. There is potential for multi–user functionality to connect people worldwide.

On usability testing
I tested with people I know on a small scale to keep costs minimal. Once a beta version of the chrome extension is ready, I will recruit a larger group to infrom the development of my design and get me to the final stage in Henrik Kniberg’s framework.
